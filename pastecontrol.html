01Termites

Termites are social insects that eat wood. There are two basic types of termites, those that live entirely in wood, and those that can tunnel into the ground (subterranean termites). The wood-inhabiting termites are the more primitive type. Among the wood inhabiting termites, there are various specialists such as rotten wood termites, damp wood termites and dry wood termites. Dry wood termites can be very serious pests of houses and furniture.
Subterranean termites are able to tunnel in the soil and can be very serious structural pests of houses and buildings. Soil inhabiting termites can also be serious pests of rangelands, tropical forestry and tropical agriculture.

Termite Facts:

  • A heavy termite infestation could mean over 200,000 termites eating you out of house and home.
  • Termites will eat almost anything. To be exact, they'll eat over 100 common household materials
  • Swarming termites are not "wood eaters," but their presence signals a need for inspection.
  • They usually only hollow out wood to obtain their food. So, wood that looks solid may actually not be.
  • A professional inspection and monitoring system is the best way to find and protect you from these hidden invaders.
  • Termites can find their way into a structure through an opening as small as 1/32 of an inch wide.
